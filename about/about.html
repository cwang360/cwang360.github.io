Technical Activities and Accomplishments

I'm currently part of the Systematic Project Organization Team (SPOT) of the Medical Robotics club at Georgia Tech. We are developing a standardized documentation system for the robotic solutions for disabilities, such as exoskeletons and prosthetics.

I'm also part of the Grand Challenges Living/Learning Community, and my team is developing a solution to help reduce downstream food waste in the United States. We have analyzed the stakeholders of the problem space and mapped out the system that affects food waste. We have detailed this process in our Medium publication. Now, we are looking at points of intervention and brainstorming solutions that will both satisfy consumers and make an impact by reducing food waste.

In the summer of 2020, I attended Google's Computer Science Summer Institute, during which I completed an introductory project-based JavaScript processing curriculum taught by Google engineers. I also attended product design, resume development, and interview workshops. Throughout the program, I configured 10 individual coding projects, as well as a collaborative final project involving an interactive weather visualizer that uses geolocation and weather APIs.

Research

Over my summers during high school, I conducted guided research on potential treatments for osteoarthritis by investigating the effects of antioxidants on cartilage under inflammatory attack. I used MATLAB to analyze calcium signaling data, and I used a click chemistry technique to track the loss of load-bearing and shock-absorbing components from cartilage extracellular matrix. I authored several abstracts that were accepted for annual Orthopaedic Research Society meetings, and I presented a poster at the 2020 meeting in Phoenix.
